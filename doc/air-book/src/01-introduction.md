# Introduction to AIR Tool

## Why It Was Developed

AI and machine learning systems potentially provide a significant and even game-changing edge to the DoD; however, their development and use is not without challenges.

In rapidly changing environments, an AI may no longer provide accurate classifications and predictions (e.g., friend vs. foe; when a system may fail; likelihood of mission success).

Distrust in their outputs can rapidly escalate into ignoring the AI's classifications and predictions. Retraining helps but takes time and is disruptive — so, when does the AI need to be retrained?

Current test and evaluation methods are inadequate for ongoing evaluation of an AI as they heavily rely on correlations within the data, which are undermined by data/concept drift, evolving edge cases, and emerging phenomena.

Following years of SEI research, the SEI has developed and tested a solution employing Causal Discovery and Causal Inference to determine when an AI's judgments are confounded.

The TAILOR project offers a precedent-setting capability that fills a gap in the DoD Test and Evaluation phase to improve the correctness of AI classifications and predictions, enhancing the use of AI in development and operations decision making.

TAILOR adds analytical capability that didn't exist, enabling an analyst to characterize and measure the overall accuracy of the AI as the underlying environment changes.

The result is a scenario-based characterization of when an AI's classifications and predictions can or cannot be trusted. In a nutshell: TAILOR helps answer whether an AI is ready to deploy.
